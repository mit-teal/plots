About the Visualization

These plots show the relation between \(f(t)\), which is the position of the end of a rope as a funciton of time, and \(psi(x,t)\), which is the amplitude of the wave generated by \(f(t)\).

Plot 1 (left):
  • (red line): the signal \(f(t)\), plotted as a function of time
  • (blue-dahsed line): a particular time \(t_{0}\), which can be varied
  • (blue dot): the amplitude \(f(t_{0})\)
Plot 2 (right):
  • (blue line): the amplitude \(\psi(x,t_{0})\) at a particular time \(t_{0}\), plotted as a function of position
  • (red dot) the red dot at the origin corresponds to the amplitude \(\psi(0,t_{0})=f(t_{0})\)
Sliders:
  • \(t_{0}\): vary the time at which the traveling wave is "observed"; this will change the vertical dashed line in Plot 1 and the shape of the wave in Plot 2.

Ideas for Exploration
