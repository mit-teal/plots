About the Visualization

These plots display the following dispersion relation: \(\omega(k)=vk\sqrt{1+\alpha k^2}\)


Plot 1 (left):
  • (black solid line) the dispersion \(\omega(k)\)
  • (black dashed line) the tangent of the dispersion curve at the wavenumber \(k_{0}\)
  • (red dashed line) the location of the wavenumber \(k_{0}\), where the tangent line is calculated

Plot 2 (right):
  • (blue line) the phase velocity, \(v_{p}\), as a function of \(k\)
  • (green dash-dotted line) the group velocity, \(v_{g}\), as a function of \(k\)
  • (red dashed line) the location of the wavenumber \(k_{0}\), where the tangent line is calculated

Sliders:
  • \(k_{0}\): vary the wavenumber, where the tangent line is calculated
  • \(v\): vary the velocity parameter
  • \(\alpha\): vary the nonlinear dispersion parameter, including negative and positive values

Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • How does the shape of the dispersion curve change as \(v\)and \(\alpha\) are varied?
  • For what values of \(\alpha\) is the phase velocity greater than the group velocity (and visa versa)?
  • Is there a set of parameters that produces a group or phase velocity equal to zero?
