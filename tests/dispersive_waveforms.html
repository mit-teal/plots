About the Visualization

Plot: Amplitude of a traveling gaussian signal as a function of position and time. The case of a dispersive medium is compared to non-dispersive medium, where the dispersion parameter \(a\) can be varied. The dispersion has the following form: $$\omega(k)=vk\sqrt{1+ak^2}$$


Sliders:

  • \(t_{0}\): vary time and observe the waveforms as they travel to the right
  • \(a\): vary the dispersion parameter from \(a=0\) to \(a=0.1\)

Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • For fixed dispersion parameter \(a\), observe how the signal disperses as function of time.
  • For fixed time, observe how different values of \(a\) affect the spread of the signal.
