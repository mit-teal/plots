About the Visualization

Plot (left): The total intensity of two superposed electric fields is shown, as a function of the phase difference between the waves.

  • Time-averaged (normalized) intensity of superposed electric fields of the following form: \(E_1(z)=A_1E_0\cos(kz)\) and \(E_2(z)=A_2E_0\cos(kz+\delta)\), where \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) are unitless coefficients.
  • Vertical cyan line shows a particular value of \(\delta\), which may be varied
  • Min and max values are also shown.

Plots (right):

  • The electric fields \(E_1(z)\) and \(E_2(z)\) are shown.
  • The phase difference \(\delta\) may be varied.


Sliders:

  • \(A_1\): vary amplitude of the wave \(E_1(z)\)
  • \(A_2\): vary amplitude of the wave \(E_2(z)\)
  • \(\delta\): vary the phase difference between the waves


Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • How does the intensity depend on both amplitudes \(A_1\) and \(A_2\)?
  • What values of \(\delta\) minimize or maximize the intensity?
  • Examine how the plots of \(E_1(z)\) and \(E_2(z)\) correspond to the intensity plot for different values of \(\delta\).
