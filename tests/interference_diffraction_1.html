About the Visualization

Plot: The time-averaged intensity of light of wavelength \(\lambda\) transmitted through two slits, with separation \(d\) and a single slit of width \(D\), and projected on a screen a distance \(L\) away. The intensity is shown as a function of \(y\,[\mathrm{mm}]\). The small angle approximation is used for the spatial window that is displayed.


Sliders:

  • \(D\,[\mathrm{\mu m}]\): vary the width of the slit.
  • \(d\,[\mathrm{\mu m}]\): vary the separation of the slits.
  • \(\lambda\,[\mathrm{nm}]\): vary the wavelength of the light.
  • \(L\,[\mathrm{m}]\): vary the distance of the screen.


Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • How does the intensity pattern depend on the parameters \(D\), \(d\), \(\lambda\), and \(L\)?
  • In what limit does the diffraction pattern "go away"?
