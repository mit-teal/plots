About the Visualization

Plot: Amplitude of a damped, driven harmonic oscillator as a function of time.

Sliders:

  • \(x_{0}\): vary intial position of the oscillator
  • \(v_{0}\): vary intial velocity of the oscillator
  • \(\omega_{d}\): vary the driving frequency of the system
  • \(\omega_{0}\): vary the natural (resonant) frequency of the system
  • \(\Gamma\): vary the damping of the system


Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • How does the amplitude depend on the parameters of the system? Does it have different behaviors at different times?
  • Identify transient behavior and steady state behavior.
  • What value of \(\omega_{d}\) leads to a resonant condition, where the amplitude is maximized?
  • For critically damped and overdamped regimes, how does the initial velocity affect the crossing point?
