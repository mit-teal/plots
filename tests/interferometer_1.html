About the Visualization

Plot (left): The classic interferometer setups is shown. An incident beam (purple) enters a beam splitter from the left and generates a rightward moving wave labeled "beam 1" (blue) and a downward moving wave labeled "beam 2" (red). The waves reflect at mirrors, whose position may be varied, and recombine at the beam splitter. The combined beam travels upwards to an area where the total intensity is depicted as a shaded color.


Sliders:

  • Beam 1 Reflector x-position: the distance of reflector 1 from the beam splitter
  • Beam 2 Reflector y-position: the distance of reflector 2 from the beam splitter


Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • How does the intensity depend on the path lengths of the beams?
  • How many wavelengths should a path length be changed in order to reproduce the same intensity?
