About the Visualization

This plot shows the reflection of an incident wave at a boundary (black vertical line). Reflection can be modeled as the superposition of identical waves traveling towards each other.


Plots:
  • (purple line, upper plot) the superposition of incident and reflected wave components
  • (black vertical line) the boundary
  • (red dot-dashed line, lower plot) the incident wave component \(f_{i}(x,t)\)
  • (blue dashed line, lower plot) the reflected (counter-propagating) wave component \(f_{r}(x,t)\)
  • The boundary condition determines "how" the incident wave component (red) and reflected wave component (blue) are superposed (either with positive or negative polarity)

Drop-down and Sliders:
  • Function: select a function from the drop-down menu.
  • Boundary: choose the boundary condition (either open or fixed)
  • \(t\): vary the time to display the functions advancing towards each other
