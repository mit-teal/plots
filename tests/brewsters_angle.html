About the Visualization

Plot (top left): Incident, reflected, and transmitted beams of light are depicted at the boundary between two media with indices of refraction \(n_1\) and \(n_2\). The incident angle can be varied. The reflected and transmitted beams are displayed with opacities that are related to their intensities relative to the incident beam. The intensities of the reflected and transmitted light depend on the indices of refraction of the media and the polarization content of the incident light, which are properties that can be varied.

Plots (right): The reflection and transmission coefficients for s-polarized light (top) and p-polarized light (middle) are shown. The total reflected and transmitted intensities of light are plotted in the bottom right plot, and the ratio of s-polarized and p-polarized light can be varied.


Sliders:

  • \(n_1\): the index of refraction of medium 1 (left)
  • \(n_2\): the index of refraction of medium 2 (right)
  • S-polarization Fraction: the relative amount of s-polarized light in the incident beam.
  • Incident Angle: the angle of the incident beam (purple) relative to the surface normal.
