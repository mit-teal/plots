About the Visualization: "Resonance"

Plot 1 (left):
  • Amplitude of a damped, driven oscillator as a function of driving frequency \(\omega_{d}\)
  • blue dashed line shows the natural frequency of the system
  • red dashed line shows the location where the amplitude has a maximum

Plot 2 (right):
  • Phase of a damped, driven oscillator as a function of driving frequency \(\omega_{d}\)
  • blue dashed line shows the natural frequency of the system
  • red dashed line shows the location where the amplitude has a maximum

Sliders:
  • \(\omega_{0}\): vary the natural (resonant) frequency of the system within the range \([0.1,1.0]\,\mathrm{Hz}\)
  • \(\Gamma\): vary the damping of the system within the range \([0.0,1.0]\,\mathrm{Hz}\)
  • \(A\): vary the amplitude of the system within the range \([0.0,10.0]\)

Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • How does the amplitude (height and width) depend on the parameters?
  • Compare the frequencies \(\omega_{0}\) and \(\omega_{\mathrm{peak}}\). How do they depend on the parameters?
  • Note the limits \(\omega_{d} \to 0\) and \(\omega_{d} \to \infty\). Does the amplitude and phase behave as expected?

Addtional Info

The complete solution ("particular" solution plus "homogeneous" solution) to the equation of motion of an underdamped, driven pendulum is:

$$ \theta(t) = A(\omega_{d})\cos(\omega_{d}t - \delta(\omega_{d})) + B e^{-\frac{\Gamma}{2}t} \cos(\omega t + \alpha) $$

where

$$ A(\omega_{d}) = \frac{f_{0}}{\sqrt{\left(\omega_{0}^{2} - \omega_{d}^{2}\right)^{2} + \omega_{d}^{2}\Gamma^{2}}} $$

and

$$ \delta(\omega_{d}) = \arctan{\frac{\omega_{d}\Gamma}{\left(\omega_{0}^{2} - \omega_{d}^{2}\right)}} $$
