About the Visualization

Plot: The time-averaged intensity of light of wavelength \(\lambda\) transmitted through two slits of separation \(d\), projected on a screen a distance \(L\) away from the slits. The intensity is shown as a function of \(y\,[\mathrm{mm}]\). The small angle approximation is used for the spatial window that is displayed.


Sliders:

  • \(d\,[\mathrm{\mu m}]\): vary the separation of the slits.
  • \(\lambda\,[\mathrm{nm}]\): vary the wavelength of the light.
  • \(L\,[\mathrm{m}]\): vary the distance of the screen.


Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • How does the intensity pattern depend on the parameters \(d\), \(\lambda\), and \(L\)?
