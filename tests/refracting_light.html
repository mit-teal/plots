About the Visualization

Plot: A wave that is incident on a boundary from the left (blue line) is refracted on the right side of the boundary (red line). The left medium has index of refraction \(n_{1}\) and the right medium has index of refraction \(n_{2}\). The direction normal to the boundary is indicated by a black-dashed line.


Sliders:

  • \(n_{1}\): vary the index of refraction of medium 1
  • \(n_{2}\): vary the index of refraction of medium 2
  • inc. angle [deg]: vary the incident angle (incident from left in medium 1)


Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • How does the angle of refraction depend on \(n_{1}\) and \(n_{2}\), for a fixed angle of incidence?
  • How does the angle of refraction depend on the angle of incidence, for fixed refractive indices?
  • What values of \(n_{1}\) and \(n_{2}\) lead to total internal reflection (i.e., no refraction)?
