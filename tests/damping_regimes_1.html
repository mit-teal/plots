About the Visualization

Plot: Amplitude of a damped harmonic oscillator as a function of time.

Sliders:

  • \(x_{0}\): vary intial position of the oscillator
  • \(v_{0}\): vary intial velocity of the oscillator
  • \(\omega_{0}\): vary the natural (resonant) frequency of the system
  • \(\Gamma\): vary the damping of the system


Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • How does the envelope (i.e., decay rate) of the amplitude depend on the parameters of the system?
  • What values of \(\omega_{0}\) and \(\Gamma\) lead to different regimes of damping (i.e., underdamped, critically damped, and overdamped)?
  • What values of \(\omega_{0}\) and \(\Gamma\) lead to the fastest amplitude decay?
  • For critically damped and overdamped regimes, how does the initial velocity affect the crossing point?
