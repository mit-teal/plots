About the Visualization

Plot: Vertical lines represent the wavelengths corresponding to constructive interference from a thin film, which depends on the index of refraction of the film \(n\) and the thickness \(d\) in the following way: \(\lambda_{\mathrm{max}}=4nd/(2N-1)\). The visible range of light is shown from \(380-740\,\mathrm{nm}\). The overall color of light within the visible range is approximated by adding the colors corresponding to constructive interference.


Sliders:

  • \(n\): vary the index of refraction of the thin film
  • \(d\): vary the thickness of the then film (in units of nm)


Ideas for Exploration

Consider the following questions and possible actions:

  • At what wavelengths does constructive interference occur for a specified \(n\) and \(d\)?
  • What values of \(n\) and \(d\) will produce one wavelength within the visible range?
  • What values of \(n\) and \(d\) will produce many wavelengths within the visible range?
